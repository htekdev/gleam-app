Brand Name Analysis

The verdict on "Gleam"

We searched trademark databases, the App Store, Google Play, and the web. Here's what we found — and what to do about it.

⚠️ Gleam — Name Conflicts Found

Caution

The name "Gleam" is appealing but carries risk in the car detailing space. Two existing businesses use it directly:

  • Gleam Detail (gleamdetail.com)
    Local car detailing company — Spanish Fork, Utah. Active domain and business. Could complicate federal trademark filing.
  • Gleam Dream Club (Google Play)
    A mobile detailing app on Google Play, Miami FL — very similar concept, same industry. Active as of Dec 2024.
  • Gleam — Generic in Car Detailing
    The word "gleam" appears in many car detailing name generator lists as a top suggestion, meaning it's a crowded, common naming choice in the industry.

Bottom Line

The opportunity is real and wide open

The on-demand car detailing market is a $48.6B industry growing at 7.2% CAGR — with the mobile segment exploding at 19.35%. No single player dominates the consumer-facing app space. Spiffy is B2B-focused. Washr and MobileWash are limited and underfunded. Traditional shops have no tech. Gleam can own the consumer-first, full-service, nationwide lane.

On the name: "Gleam" is memorable and on-brand but has some naming conflicts in the detailing industry. We recommend filing a trademark immediately or pivoting to Luster, Shyne, or Buffr for stronger IP protection. The concept is strong regardless of the final name.
